Our Story

Born From a Bold Idea
in Uganda

C360 Innovation Lab was founded in 2020 by a group of educators, entrepreneurs, and community leaders who saw a widening gap — Uganda's youth were full of potential, but lacked structured pathways to turn ideas into real impact.

Starting with a single innovation club in Kampala, C360 grew rapidly. Word spread that something different was happening — a space where young people were not just taught, but challenged, mentored, and given real tools to build.

Today, C360 operates across multiple districts in Uganda, running 40+ active clubs, engaging 80+ expert mentors, and supporting hundreds of young innovators on their journey from idea to enterprise.

We didn't build C360 to teach young people about innovation. We built it so they could live it — every single day.

— C360 Founding Team, 2020

Our Mission

Empowering Youth to Innovate Today

To nurture Uganda's young talent by providing structured innovation clubs, expert mentorship, enterprise development support, and financial literacy training — equipping them with the skills, networks, and confidence to solve real problems and build sustainable livelihoods.

Our Vision

A Uganda Led by Its Own Innovators

A Uganda where every young person — regardless of background or location — has access to the tools, mentors, and opportunities needed to turn their ideas into enterprises that drive economic growth, community development, and lasting change across Africa.

Milestones

Our Journey So Far

From a single club in Kampala to a nationwide movement — every step that brought us here.

2020 — Q1
C360 Innovation Lab Founded

A small team of educators and entrepreneurs came together in Kampala with a shared vision: give Uganda's youth a real platform to innovate.

2020 — Q3
First Innovation Club Launched

The Tech Innovators Club opened its doors in Kampala with 18 founding members — our very first cohort of young builders.

2021 — Q1
First Partner Onboarded

Makerere University became C360's first institutional partner, opening access to research facilities and academic mentors.

2021 — Q3
Expanded to 5 Districts

Clubs launched in Gulu, Jinja, Entebbe, and Mbarara — taking C360 beyond Kampala for the first time.

2022 — Q2
100 Members Milestone

C360 crossed 100 active youth members — a turning point that proved the model worked and demand was real.

2022 — Q4
First National Innovation Challenge

C360 hosted Uganda's first youth-led national innovation challenge, attracting 200+ entries from 12 districts.

2023 — Q2
UNDP & GIZ Partnerships Secured

Two landmark global partnerships unlocked new funding, resources, and international visibility for C360 programs.

2024 — Q1
500+ Members & 40+ Clubs

C360 reached 500 active youth members across 40+ clubs — cementing its place as Uganda's leading youth innovation platform.

2025 — Now
Digital Platform Launched

C360 goes online — bringing clubs, mentorship, resources, and enterprise tools to every young Ugandan with a phone.
